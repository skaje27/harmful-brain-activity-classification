Welcome to Harmful Brain Activity Classification



In the realm of healthcare, the monitoring and management of neurological disorders, particularly in critically ill patients, demand meticulous attention and timely intervention. Electroencephalography (EEG) stands as a pivotal tool in this domain, enabling the capture of intricate brain activity patterns essential for detecting and categorizing detrimental occurrences like seizures.

However, the current landscape of EEG analysis relies heavily on manual inspection by specialized neurologists, creating inefficiencies, susceptibility to fatigue-induced errors, and variability across different reviewers.

Our project seeks to catalyze a paradigm shift by automating EEG analysis, presenting a revolutionary solution poised to rapidly and accurately detect and classify seizures and other harmful brain activities in critically ill patients.

Leveraging a comprehensive dataset comprising EEG recordings, matched spectrograms, and detailed annotations, our initiative aims to provide a robust foundation for training advanced machine learning models.

By harnessing the power of automation and machine learning, we endeavor not only to streamline EEG analysis processes but also to significantly improve patient care outcomes in critical care settings.
